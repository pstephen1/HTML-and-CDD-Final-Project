What is FDM 3D Printing?


To start, we should define what 3D printing is, and in particular, we will touch on FDM 3D printing, the most common method that we use.  3DHubs defines FDM 3D printing as:

Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM), or Fused Filament Fabrication (FFF), is an additive manufacturing process that belongs to the material extrusion family. In FDM, an object is built by selectively depositing melted material in a pre-determined path layer-by-layer. The materials used are thermoplastic polymers and come in a filament form.

So what does this mean?  Molten filament is pressed through a nozzle and "squished" into extremely thin layers.  The printer keeps drawing layers on top of one another until an object is made, which can be anything you want - A figure, a toy, missing battery cover, bread slicing guides, if it can be made with plastic then an FDM 3D printer can make it!
